### 2. TONE & DIPLOMACY: **B (82/100)**

**Strengths:**

- Generally professional and respectful tone
- Good use of "we" language that includes the reader
- Empathetic acknowledgment of workload concerns
- Clear without being condescending

**Areas Needing Improvement:**

**Issue 1: Liability Section Tone (Lines 1322-1599)**

- The liability section, while accurate, can feel defensive or accusatory
- Example: "You won't be named in a DOJ lawsuit, but..." creates unnecessary fear
- Recommendation: Reframe to emphasize positive professional responsibilities rather than legal consequences

**Issue 2: Some Managerial Language**

- Line 1367: "Don't create impossible deadlines" could be more diplomatic
- Line 1376: "Don't approve content that doesn't meet WCAG 2.1 Level AA" is direct but could be softened
- Recommendation: Use more collaborative language ("Work with staff to ensure..." vs. "Don't approve...")

**Issue 3: "Why Trust This FAQ" Section (Lines 371-443)**

- While necessary, this section is somewhat defensive
- The repeated emphasis on "web developer not on executive staff" creates unnecessary hierarchy
- Recommendation: Shorten and reframe to focus on sources rather than author credentials

**Issue 4: Some Absolute Statements**

- "The deadline is firm" appears multiple times (necessary but could vary phrasing)
- "It's not optional" appears frequently (accurate but could be softened)
- Recommendation: Vary language while maintaining clarity

**Recommendations:**

1. **High Priority:** Reframe liability section to emphasize professional responsibilities and support rather than legal consequences
2. **Medium Priority:** Soften managerial directives to be more collaborative
3. **Medium Priority:** Shorten and reframe "Why Trust This FAQ" section
4. **Low Priority:** Vary language around requirements/deadlines to reduce repetition

### 3. STRUCTURE & ORGANIZATION: **B (85/100)**

**Strengths:**

- Good use of "Quick Reference" at the top
- Logical flow from general to specific
- "Getting Started" section provides good entry points

**Areas Needing Improvement:**

**Issue 1: Question Organization Within Sections**

**"General Accessibility Questions" Section (Lines 76-543):**

- Contains 15 questions covering diverse topics
- Mixes foundational questions with specific concerns
- Recommendation: Split into subsections:
  - Foundational Questions (What is accessibility? What is WCAG?)
  - Compliance Questions (Deadlines, requirements, scope)
  - Getting Help Questions (Who to ask, training resources)

**"Compliance Requirements" Section (Lines 1159-1320):**

- Well-organized but could benefit from clearer subsections
- IITAA and ADA Title II questions are mixed
- Recommendation: Add clear subsections:
  - IITAA Requirements
  - ADA Title II Requirements
  - Exceptions and Exemptions

**"Content Accessibility" Section (Lines 2017-2514):**

- Very long section (497 lines) covering multiple document types
- Recommendation: Split into subsections:
  - General Document Accessibility
  - Word Documents
  - PDFs
  - PowerPoint Presentations
  - Excel Spreadsheets
  - Email and Newsletters

**Issue 2: Redundant Questions**

- "Why is this FAQ so long?" (Line 86) and "Why does accessibility compliance require everyone's participation?" (Line 179) overlap significantly
- "What happens after April 24, 2026?" (Line 938) and "What if we can't meet the deadline?" (Line 977) could be combined
- Recommendation: Consolidate or cross-reference more explicitly

**Issue 3: Cross-References**

- Good use of "See also" links, but could be expanded
- Some related questions aren't cross-referenced
- Recommendation: Add more internal links between related topics

**Recommendations:**

1. **High Priority:** Split "General Accessibility Questions" into logical subsections
2. **High Priority:** Split "Content Accessibility" section into document-type subsections
3. **Medium Priority:** Consolidate redundant questions or add explicit cross-references
4. **Medium Priority:** Add more internal cross-references between related questions
